BigQuery Disk Usage (BQdu)

Visualize Google BigQuery's Storage

Welcome to BQdu

If you're using Google BigQuery as part of your daily work, chances you're asking yourself questions like 'How much data do I have in BigQuery?' or 'Which datasets hold the most data in my project' or maybe even 'What tables are largest within my dataset' all the time.

Inspired by Linux's 'du', BQdu was created as small service which does exactly what 'du' does for folders but only for Google BigQuery's projects, datasets and tables.

BQdu will scan your project/s and display a treemap visualization of your project, datasets and tables. You can mouse-over each tile and get a nice tooltip with information about the entity you're pointing to. Tiles are sized according to their relative size compared to the dataset (and datasets to projects).

Want to give it a run? You can access BQdu at bqdu.info

FAQ

  • The app will ask you to login. Due to nuances of BigQuery API, it will ask you to give it access to your BigQuery project. More info.
  • Running the app will not increase your BigQuery billing. BQdu just reads your BigQuery metadata and therefore it's 100% free
